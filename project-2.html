#2. Experiment 2 — RunwayML Gen-4 Agentic Organism Growth

I prompted on RunwayML Gen-4 to reimagine an art piece I made as a living microorganism. This generates a video, then my recursive python script takes a screenshot of the generation and runs the prompt repeatedly — up to 3 times for now — as an act of "feeding and growing" the piece.

All content uses only my original visuals and reference inputs of microscopic lab photos of microcellular organisms.

✓  What worked
Fusing two artworks as the starting seed
Starting from a blend of two drawings produced more interesting organisms than starting from one alone. The creature inherited something from both sources — you could feel both artworks in the result.
Introducing a reference photo mid-growth
Using the same organism seed but pointing growth toward different reference images produced clearly distinct results. The same creature became a glowing botanical garden in one direction and a parasitic ruin mutation in another.
Each artwork's palette shaped its organism
The same animation prompt applied to all 7 source artworks produced 7 visually distinct creatures. The colour and form of the original drawing carried through into the organism's character.
✗  What to push further
The organisms read as abstract, not biological
The results are visually compelling but don't quite read as real microorganisms. Shifting the prompt toward dark-field microscopy framing brought the results closer to convincing microbiology.
Generations plateau without new input
After four growth cycles on the same chain, each new generation looks similar to the last. Injecting a reference photo is what keeps the evolution moving — without it, the organism stops changing.
Interpolating between organisms doesn't work as expected
Attempting to smoothly transition between two very different organisms produced a hallucinated blend rather than a true morph. Simple crossfade and motion-flow alternatives were tested but both read as a dissolve, not a transformation.
All images used in the experiments are available stock photos (microcells) and artworks created by me.
