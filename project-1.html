The Movement of Memory, Solo Art Show
New York City, Oct 2024

In "The Movement of Memory", Emily invites you to explore fragments of her past, translating the brain's process of memory recall into physical art. These prints, created from her own photo works and enhanced with AI technique called Frame Interpolation, offer a glimpse into the interplay between technology and the human experience.

On October 20th, 2024, I put together an AI Art show inside a Contemporary Coffee Shop showcasing prints on canvas as well as my past photography work. The art pieces were generated through AI experimentation with my own photos from key moments of my life. Considering the limited time and budget to put together the show in a month, I focused closely on the concept I wanted to present to my spectators — how the human brain recalls memories as mental imagery.

Frame Interpolation

  • The algorithm studies every pixel in one frame (still image), tracks how it shifts in the next, and then invents a "mid-step" frame: unmoving areas stay as-is, while only the parts in motion are newly generated
  • academy.runwayml.com/ai-tools/frame-interpolation
